Wine Classification

In my wine classification project, I undertook a comprehensive approach to develop a sophisticated machine learning model capable of accurately classifying different varieties of wine based on their chemical composition. The dataset used contained a plethora of features, such as alcohol content, acidity levels, and phenolic compounds, which were meticulously preprocessed to ensure data quality and relevance. This involved handling missing values, normalizing the data, and performing feature selection to identify the most influential attributes. I explored multiple classification algorithms, including k-Nearest Neighbors, Decision Trees, and Support Vector Machines, each offering unique advantages in terms of interpretability and performance. Through iterative experimentation and hyperparameter tuning, I ultimately selected a Random Forest classifier, which provided the highest accuracy and robustness. The final model demonstrated its effectiveness with a precision rate exceeding industry standards, highlighting its potential application in the wine industry for quality control and product differentiation. This project not only honed my technical skills in data preprocessing, algorithm selection, and model evaluation but also provided a deep dive into the fascinating intersection of machine learning and viticulture.
